THE GREATEST EVER

The greatest ever cricketer to live. One of the finest batsman to grace the game alongside modern greats like Sachin Tendulkar, Sir WG Grace but he was the in the league of his own.

    A short timeline of the greatest.

  • 1908: - On 27 August is when the maestro was born in New South Wales in a small town of Cootamundra. He had a brother and three sisters. Bradman was of English heritage of both sides of the family.
  • 1911: - When he was two and a half years old his family relocated themselves to Bowral to be close to their family friends.
  • 1920: - First he acted as a scorer for the local team then in October the team was one man short so they picked Bradman and he scored 37* and 29*.
  • 1925-26:- He played Regularly for Bowral and was scoring tons of runs that it brought attention to high members and also for the Sydney press
  • 1927: - Bradman, at the age of 19 made his first-class debut for NSW Team at the Adelaide Oval.
  • 1928-29: - England toured Austailia but Bradman contiuned working in real estate than later took a job with a sporting goods company.
  • 1928: - The time when Bradman made his test debut against England, which wasnot a good debut for him as the whole team was thrashed against England as they were all out for 66 and lost by an innings and 675 runs.
  • 1930: - The Ashes was held and th Austrailian team went to England. This was the first time Bradman went abroad to play, there he scored 131 in his first test in England. In the second test he scored 254 which Austrailia won. He later described his 254 as his best innings:
    • "practically without exception every ball went where it was intended to go".
  • 1930-31: - The series against West Indies his scoring was more sedate but he did score 223 and 152.
  • 1932-33: - The most famous series of all time THE BODYLINE series where sparked numerous controversies that ended promising careers.
